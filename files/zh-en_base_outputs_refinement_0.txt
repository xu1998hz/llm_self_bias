The International Olive Oil Council classifies olive oil into two main categories: virgin olive oil and refined olive oil, with five grades.
Extra virgin olive oil, also known as natural olive oil, is obtained directly from fresh olive fruits through mechanical cold pressing, filtration, and other treatments to remove impurities.
According to different acidity levels, it can be divided into three levels:
Extra Virgin Olive Oil: It is the highest grade and highest quality olive oil, and is a pure natural product.
Excellent taste, with a subtle and pleasant plant fragrance, acidity not exceeding 1%.
High-quality virgin olive oil (Fine Virgin): Slightly higher acidity, but not exceeding 2%, pure and fragrant taste.
Ordinary Extra Virgin Olive Oil: The taste and flavor are acceptable, with acidity not exceeding 3.3%.
Refined olive oil refers to olive oil obtained after refining extra virgin olive oil with an acidity level exceeding 3.3%, or it is also known as "second-grade oil".
Refined olive oil can be divided into two grades:
Regular Olive Oil: Refined olive oil mixed with a certain proportion of extra virgin olive oil to balance flavor and color. It has an acidity level below 1.5% and appears as a transparent light golden yellow color.
Refined Olive-Pomace Oil: It is obtained by extracting and refining olive oil from pomace through a dissolving method.
You should have noticed, after viewing the classification of grades, that the acidity of olive oil is a very important indicator.
To know, the olive fruit is just like grapes used for making wine. Olive fruits from different production areas and different olive fruit varieties will produce olive oil with different standards.
The best growing place for olive trees:
The Mediterranean coast has the most suitable soil and climate conditions, including temperature, sunlight, air humidity, and precipitation, for the growth of olive trees.
Apart from the Mediterranean coast, other regions can only sporadically grow in suitable habitats.
Main producing regions (Mediterranean):
Italy, Spain, Greece, Turkey, Portugal, Morocco, Syria, Israel, Tunisia, Algeria, Egypt, Pakistan, Lebanon, etc., and there are also Argentina, Peru, South Africa, etc., which have similar climates even though they are located far south.
The harvesting month of olive oil:
From March to June every year is the time when olive trees bloom and bear fruit. From July to October, the fruits begin to develop and ripen, transitioning from green to red, then purple, and finally turning black.
November to February is the harvest season for fruits (the freshness of olive oil is very important).
Nutritional components of olive oil:
Olive oil is rich in abundant monounsaturated fatty acids, namely oleic acid, linoleic acid, and linolenic acid, as well as vitamins A, B, D, E, K, and antioxidants.
Olive oil is considered to be the most suitable oil for human nutrition among all the oils discovered so far.
