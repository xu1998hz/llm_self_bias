The International Olive Oil Council classifies olive oil into two categories: virgin olive oil and refined olive oil, with five different grades.
Extra virgin olive oil, also known as natural olive oil, is obtained by mechanically cold pressing fresh olives and then filtering to remove impurities.
According to the different acidity levels, it can be divided into three levels:
Extra Virgin Olive Oil: It is the highest grade and highest quality olive oil, and it is a pure natural product.
Excellent taste, with a delicate and pleasant plant fragrance, acidity not exceeding 1%.
High-quality extra virgin olive oil (Fine Virgin): Slightly high acidity, but not exceeding 2%, with a pure and fragrant taste.
Ordinary Virgin Olive Oil: The taste and flavor are acceptable, with an acidity level not exceeding 3.3%.
Refined olive oil refers to the olive oil obtained after refining virgin olive oil with acidity exceeding 3.3%, or known as "second oil".
Refined olive oil can be divided into two grades:
Regular olive oil (Olive Oil): Refined olive oil blended with a certain proportion of extra virgin olive oil to balance taste and color. Its acidity is below 1.5%, and it has a transparent light golden color.
Refined Olive-Pomace Oil: It is obtained by extracting and refining olive oil from the olive pomace through a dissolution method.
You should have noticed after reading the classification that acidity is a very important indicator for olive oil.
You should know that olives are like grapes used to make wine. Olives from different regions and different varieties of olives will produce olive oil with different standards.
The best growth place for olive oil:
The Mediterranean coast has the most suitable soil and climatic conditions, such as temperature, sunlight, air humidity, and precipitation, for olive tree growth.
Apart from the Mediterranean coast, sporadic planting can only be done in other areas within the suitable habitat zone.
Main production area (Mediterranean):
Italy, Spain, Greece, Turkey, Portugal, Morocco, Syria, Israel, Tunisia, Algeria, Egypt, Pakistan, Lebanon, and so on, also have similar climates, as well as countries far south such as Argentina, Peru, and South Africa.
Harvest month of olive oil:
Every year from March to June is the time for olive trees to bloom and bear fruit. From July to October, the fruits start to grow and ripen, transitioning from green to red, purple, and finally black.
November to February is the harvest season for fruits (the freshness of olive oil is very important).
Nutritional components of olive oil:
Olive oil is rich in monounsaturated fatty acids, including oleic acid and linoleic acid, as well as vitamin A, vitamin B, vitamin D, vitamin E, vitamin K, and antioxidants.
Olive oil is considered to be the most suitable oil for human nutrition among the oils discovered to date.
